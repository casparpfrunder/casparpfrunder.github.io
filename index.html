About

Jurist and Historian

I am a jurist and historian working at the intersection of legal theory, intellectual history, and political economy. My work traces how ideas, institutions and culture shape societies and how knowledge about nature and man is produced — from antiquity to the present day.

My doctoral dissertation, defended at the University of St. Gallen in 2025, anaylzes the emergence and spread of constitutionalism as a global phenomenon. It spans antiquity to modernity with comparative cases in the United Kingdom, United States, France, and Switzerland.

I hold a PhD in the Social Sciences from the Universiy of St. Gallen, an MPhil in Modern European History from the University of Oxford and am completing a Master of Laws at HSG.
